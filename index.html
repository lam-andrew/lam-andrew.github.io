01 · About

Software and AI engineering, grid-scale

I'm a software and AI engineer at Southern California Edison, one of the largest electric utilities in the country. My work sits at the intersection of software engineering and applied AI: building retrieval-augmented (RAG) chatbots over confidential internal knowledge, standing up on-prem GenAI infrastructure, and modeling the grid as a graph-based digital twin in Neo4j.

I like leading and architecting projects end to end, from the data model to the interface engineers actually use in the field. Working next to critical infrastructure means I care as much about security and reliability as I do about shipping fast.

Outside of work I'm finishing a Master of Software Engineering at Penn State, and I run Nuppin Studio, a design and 3D-printing studio making modern objects for the home and desk. The through line: I like building complete things, from the model to the pipeline to the object in your hand.

06 · Beyond the keyboard

The maker side

Nuppin Studio

A small design and 3D-printing studio based in California, creating modern objects for your home, desk, and everyday life. From minimalist trays and vases to playful desk pieces, every item is made to order with a focus on clean, intentional design.

The studio is also growing a collection of digital designs: downloadable files you can print yourself at home, so you can bring the designs to life on your own terms. Whether you want something handmade or something to make yourself, it's a one-person operation covering the whole pipeline: modeling in Blender, print tuning on a Bambu Lab P1S, finishing, photography, and the storefront itself.
